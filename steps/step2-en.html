Step 2 – Centralized or Decentralized?

You know your wallet. Now decide where you operate: centralized platforms (CEX) vs. decentralized protocols (DEX), plus a short glossary. You choose whom to trust — a provider or the code.

← Back to Step 1

Why this step matters

Centralized means: you use someone. Decentralized means: you use the code. Both have a place. What counts is who holds the keys — and how much responsibility you take.

Mentor: “If you log in instead of signing, you’re in a centralized system.”

Centralized platforms (CEX)

You sign up with email/password, pass KYC, and the provider holds your coins in their system. You see a balance — but the coins aren’t in your own wallet.

Typical advantages

  • Convenience, app UX, support
  • Easy FIAT deposits (SEPA/card)
  • Broad market access (per provider)

Typical drawbacks

  • Data & custody handed over (KYC, custodian risk)
  • Counterparty/outage risk
  • Fees/spreads depending on provider

Examples (not endorsements)

  • Trustyfy — bridge from bank account to chain (IBAN, KYC, focus on in/out)
  • Crypto.com — app-based, card/cashback, many coins
  • Binance — huge selection & liquidity, more complex UI
  • Coinbase / Bitpanda — beginner-friendly, often higher fees
Always check withdrawal routes to your own wallet. “Not your keys, not your coins.”

Decentralized world (DEX & wallets)

No account, no customer file — you connect your wallet to a smart contract. Swaps run peer-to-contract, transparently on-chain. Control stays with you — so does responsibility.

DEX examples

  • Uniswap (Ethereum) — largest DEX
  • PancakeSwap (BNB Chain) — similar, often cheaper
  • 1inch / Jupiter / Osmosis — aggregators/DEX across networks

Wallet gateway (MEW example)

  • MEW – MyEtherWallet: non-custodial (Ethereum)
  • Direct wallet connect; swaps via integrated routes
  • No KYC — you sign → full responsibility
Decentralized ≠ “safety net”. It’s honest: you are your own support.

Bridges & hybrids in practice

Many combine: centralized entry (Trustyfy/CEX) → withdraw to your wallet → decentralized usage (Uniswap/MEW). Lines blur — the key question remains who holds the keys.

  • CEX buy → on-chain transfer → DEX swap
  • Wallet-connect from a CEX app to DEX services (hybrids)

Glossary — short & clear

TermMeaning
KYCKnow Your Customer — identity verification at centralized services.
CEXCentralized exchange — provider-run marketplace with account access.
DEXDecentralized exchange — smart contracts + wallet signature.
Smart ContractProgram on a blockchain that enforces rules automatically.
Gas FeeNetwork fee for transactions (e.g., Ethereum).
Liquidity PoolShared token pool powering DEX swaps.
StablecoinFiat-pegged token (USDT, USDC). Stabilizing but not risk-free.
Seed Phrase12–24 words to restore your wallet. Never store digitally.
SwapDirect token exchange, usually on a DEX.
Fiat BridgeService bringing bank money on-chain (Trustyfy, CEX deposits).
Non-custodialOnly you control the keys (MEW, hardware wallets).
