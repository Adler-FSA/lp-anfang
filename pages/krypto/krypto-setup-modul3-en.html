Module 3 – Decentral Setup (Wallets & Keys)

You build your first decentralized setup – with clear wallet roles, solid seed backups and simple rules so you don’t lose track.

After this module you know which wallets you use for what, where your keys live and how you would recover if you lost a device or access.

Step 1

Mini scenario: “Phone gone – is my crypto gone too?”

You’re on a train, your phone slips out of your pocket – gone. On that phone: your main crypto wallet holding a four-figure balance. You never see the device again. Question: Is the money gone – or do you have a plan?

Three key questions:

  • Do you have a properly written seed stored anywhere right now?
  • Do you know which amounts sit on which wallets?
  • Could you realistically rebuild everything on a new device within 24–48 hours?

Mini task: “If I lost my main device tomorrow – what would my realistic loss be in € / $?”

Step 2

Core idea: Not an app, but keys

An exchange is like a bank account: you have a login, but not the vault key. A self-custody wallet manages your private keys. Whoever controls the keys controls the money – regardless of the device.

Key phrase:
“Password = access to a platform. Seed = access to the money.”

Reflection: “Where is my focus today – mostly platform passwords or real keys I control?”

Step 3

Wallet roles: Three layers instead of one big pot

The goal of this module is not “one wallet for everything”, but clear roles. The structure is a suggestion you can adapt to your situation.

3.1 Everyday wallet (“pocket wallet”)

  • Mobile wallet on your phone.
  • Small amounts for tests, small payments, early DeFi experiments.
  • If this goes to zero, it’s annoying – but not life-threatening.

  • Medium-sized holdings you don’t move every day.
  • Hardware wallet or dedicated software wallet on a clean device.
  • Goal: sovereignty with reserves, not constant moving around.

3.3 Long-term / archive wallet (“vault”)

  • Holdings meant for many years.
  • As offline as possible (second hardware wallet, paper wallet, special setup).
  • Access only via a clear, deliberate procedure – intentionally inconvenient.
Mentor impulse:
“The longer money is meant to sit still, the less convenient it should be to touch it.”

Step 4

Seeds & backups: Your recovery chain

4.1 Creating a proper seed backup

Checklist for your seed backup:

Seed written down offline only (paper, steel, dedicated backup medium).
No screenshots, no cloud notes, no photos.
All words double-checked – correctly spelled and clearly readable.

Mini task: “Create two readable seed copies today – with date and clear label for which wallet they belong to.”

4.2 A simple 3-2-1 backup model

Adapted rule of thumb:

  • 3 copies of your seed(s),
  • 2 different locations,
  • 1 location with extra protection against fire/water.
