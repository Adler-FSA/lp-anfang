Module 7 – Fine-Tuning, Growth & Annual Review

Step 1

Mini scenario: “One year later – now what?”

One year has passed since you built your crypto setup:

  • Your income situation has changed.
  • Regulation in your country has moved forward.
  • Some projects disappeared, others grew significantly.

Your setup is alive — but does it still fit you?

Question: “If you look at it honestly today, does your setup feel more like a consciously maintained garden — or more like an overgrown backyard where things just happen to grow?”

Step 2

Looking back: what should your setup actually do?

Quick recap of the core goals from Modules 1–6:

  • Module 1: clarity about your starting point and dependencies.
  • Module 2: minimum protection — 1–3 monthly budgets decentralized.
  • Module 3: structure and distribution (centralized/decentralized, regions, technology).
  • Module 4: protection from seizures, blocks, and overreach.
  • Module 5: documentation and handover (for yourself + a trusted person).
  • Module 6: daily routines and warning signals.

Guiding question in Module 7: “Does my setup still meet at least these goals today — and if yes, where can I calmly refine it?”

Step 3

The annual review: one big cleanup, not constant stress

Module 7 introduces one deliberate yearly appointment:

“Once per year — 60 to 120 minutes — I do a full setup review.”

3.1 Preparation

Recommended structure for your annual review:

  • Quiet place, phone on silent.
  • Laptop/tablet plus your documents from Modules 1–5.
  • Protected time block: “Right now I only do setup review, nothing else.”

Preparation checklist:

  • Open your overview (accounts, wallets, platforms, seeds).
  • Have your current financial situation roughly in mind.
  • Optional: mentor or partner contact for later follow-up questions.

How and where do you want to anchor your yearly review in real life?

Step 4

Step 1 of the annual review: take stock

You go through things point by point:

  • 1. Where is how much stored?
    • centralized fiat
    • centralized exchanges
    • decentralized wallets
    • hardware wallets
  • 2. Does the distribution still make sense?
    • Is one platform overweighted?
    • Do you hold assets you no longer understand or no longer want to follow?
  • 3. Minimum protection check:
    • Are 1–3 monthly budgets still accessible in decentralized form?
    • Has your monthly need changed?

Mini task: “Mark 1–3 areas where you see too much concentration or too much chaos.”

Step 5

Step 2: clean up, simplify, focus

This is not about adding more complexity, but about: “Less, but more conscious.”

Typical questions:

  • Do I really still need all these exchanges / apps?
  • Are there legacy leftovers I can close or empty?
  • Are there tiny balances I want to consolidate?

Examples:

  • You have 5 exchanges but actively use only 2 → consciously close 1–2.
  • You still hold coins you no longer follow → decide clearly: keep them on purpose or exit.
  • You have multiple devices that are only “half integrated” → define roles: main device, backup device.

Mini task: “Make 1–3 concrete simplification decisions for your setup. No perfectionism — three clean steps are better than 20 open construction sites.”

Step 6

Step 3: tighten security & handover

The annual review is also the moment to update the topics from Modules 4 & 5:

  • Emergency access
    • Has anything changed with your trusted person (relationship, reachability, location)?
    • Do your instructions need updating (new wallets, new priorities)?
  • Technical changes
    • New hardware (phone, laptop, hardware wallet)?
    • Do seeds, PINs, or 2FA codes need adjustment to new devices?

Mini task: “Write a short 2–3 line emergency update for your trusted person: what changed, and where the current version of your overview is stored.”

Step 7

Step 4: plan growth consciously (not by accident)

Instead of “let’s see what happens,” ask yourself things like:

  • What percentage of my total net worth should be in crypto long term?
  • What portion of that should be held in decentralized self-custody?
  • Which projects or assets do I actively follow — and which are only passively running along?

Guiding question: “When I look back at this moment next year — what exactly should be better? (1–3 points)”

Step 8

Step 5: your personal setup statement

To close Module 7, you create a short setup statement. Ideally, your setup stands on these pillars:

Minimum protection (x monthly budgets decentralized).
Diversification (centralized/decentralized, regions, technology).
Emergency plan for yourself.
Handover plan for at least one trusted person.
Clear routines and one annual review appointment.

Turn this into 3–5 sentences, for example: “I have built a conscious crypto setup that protects my core needs, does not depend on a single platform, and remains understandable in an emergency.”

Step 9

Concrete tasks in Module 7

To-do block for your annual review:

Set the annual appointment: choose date and time and put it in your calendar (“Annual Crypto Setup Review”).
Run the inventory: document the current distribution and mark 1–3 areas of concentration or chaos.
Simplify: make 1–3 concrete cleanup decisions (platforms, coins, devices).
Update emergency and handover info: write a short note about what changed and where the latest overview is stored.
Write your setup statement: a short personal text describing what your system stands for.
