Step 3

Seeds & wallets ‚Äì secure, but not lost

3.1 Typical seed mistakes

Common pitfalls:

  • Seed on a single sheet of paper in a drawer, with no protection.
  • Seed only inside a password manager, no physical backup.
  • Seed photographed and stored in the cloud.
  • Seed hidden in such a ‚Äúsmart‚Äú way that even you might not find it later.

Reflection: ‚ÄúIf you had to restore your seed in 2 years ‚Äì with less memory than today ‚Äì would you find it and understand it?‚Äù

3.2 Backup strategy: simple and robust

The focus is on principles, not brands:

  • At least one physical backup outside your main residence.
  • Clear format: readable, complete, not half-encrypted riddles.
  • Separation of seed and hints (no ‚ÄúLedger seed‚Äù sticky note right next to it).

Typical building blocks: paper backup (easy, fragile), metal backup (durable, visible), or a combination of both.

Step 4

Passwords, 2FA & email ‚Äì your master key

4.1 Email as the real root access

Your primary email account is often the true root:

  • Password resets.
  • 2FA backup codes.
  • Account recovery links.

So you need a strong, unique password, 2FA (ideally app-based) and backup codes stored in a secure place.

4.2 Password strategy

No product promotion ‚Äì just a logic you can stick to:

  • No reuse of the same password across crypto, email and banking.
  • Clear separation between everyday passwords and critical ones.
  • A system you can follow long term.
