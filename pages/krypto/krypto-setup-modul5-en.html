Step 2

Three layers of security: Device ‚Äì Access ‚Äì Human

To keep things clear, you structure your security concept into three layers:

2.1 Device layer

  • Phone, laptop, hardware wallets, USB sticks.
  • Questions: Are they encrypted? Is there PIN/password protection?
  • Could a thief just start using them if they found the device?

2.2 Access layer

  • Passwords, PINs, 2FA apps, your email inbox as ‚Äúmaster key‚Äù.
  • Questions: Is there one email account that can reset almost everything?
  • Are your passwords truly unique or only small variations?

2.3 Human layer

  • You under stress, family, trusted people.
  • Questions: Does anyone know you even have a wallet?
  • Is there a clean way for someone to step in if you’re in hospital or unable to act?
Key phrase:
‚ÄúSecurity is not only tech ‚Äì it’s mainly clarity about who can do what in an emergency.‚Äù

Step 3

Seeds & wallets ‚Äì secure, but not lost

3.1 Typical seed mistakes

Module 5 builds on Module 3 and goes deeper into the backup topic. Typical pitfalls:

  • Seed on a single paper sheet in a drawer with no protection.
  • Seed only in a password manager with no physical backup.
  • Seed photographed and stored in the cloud.
  • Seed hidden in such a ‚Äúsmart‚Äù way that even you might not find it later.

Reflection: ‚ÄúIf you had to restore your seed in 2 years ‚Äì with less memory than today ‚Äì would you find it and understand it?‚Äù

3.2 Backup strategy: simple and robust

The course doesn’t push any brand, just principles:

  • At least one physical backup outside your main residence.
  • Clear format: readable, complete, not half-encrypted riddles.
  • Separation of seed and labels (no ‚ÄúLedger seed for wallet XY‚Äù sticky right next to it).

Typical building blocks: paper backup, metal backup, a combination with parts of the information at a second location.

Mini task: ‚ÄúDescribe in one sentence how your main seed is stored today ‚Äì and what would happen to it in a house fire.‚Äù

Step 4

Passwords, 2FA & email ‚Äì your master key

4.1 Email as the real root access

Your primary email account is often the true ‚Äúroot‚Äù:

  • Password resets for many services.
  • 2FA backup codes and recovery links.
  • Communication hub for banks, exchanges and tools.

So you need a strong, unique password here, 2FA (preferably app-based) and securely stored backup codes.

4.2 Password strategy

Again, no product promotion ‚Äì just logic you can stick to long term:

  • No reuse of the same password across crypto, email and banking.
  • Clear separation between everyday passwords (forums, newsletters ‚Ä¶) and critical passwords (email, bank, exchanges, wallet tools).
  • A system you will still understand in five years.

Mini task: ‚ÄúIn your private notes, list three accounts whose passwords you want to review or upgrade first (e.g. main email, mobile bank, biggest exchange).‚Äù

Emergency access for trusted people

Module 5 also asks: ‚ÄúWhat happens to your funds if something happens to you?‚Äù This is not legal advice, but practical options.

  • That person knows roughly where information can be found (e.g. sealed envelope, emergency folder with hints).
  • References can live in your important documents or ‚Äì where legally appropriate ‚Äì in your will.
