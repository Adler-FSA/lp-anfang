5 Crypto Setup – Module

Module 5 – Security Concept & Backups

You build a security concept that doesn’t just look good on paper, but works in real stress situations: device loss, hacks, fire, hospital stays or longer downtime.

After this module you know where your real weak spots are, how to secure seeds, passwords and devices – and how to run an emergency restore without panic.

Step 1

Mini scenario: “Your digital life in a box”

Imagine your home doesn’t burn down completely – but the room with your devices is unusable. Laptop gone, phone gone, maybe even some paper notes damaged.

You need access to money, communication and accounts. What can you reconstruct from memory – and what disappears with the hardware?

Key questions:

  • Which three items would you need today to rebuild your digital life?
  • Are those only stored on a single device?
  • Is there anyone who could help you in a real emergency – but has no idea where to start?

Mini task: “Write down – without checking – where your main seed is stored, where your most important passwords are backed up, and which device is a single point of failure for you.”

Step 2

Three layers of security: Device – Access – Human

To keep things clear, you structure your security concept into three layers:

2.1 Device layer

  • Phone, laptop, hardware wallets, USB sticks.
  • Questions: Are they encrypted? Is there PIN or password protection?
  • Could a thief just start using them if they found the device?

2.2 Access layer

  • Passwords, PINs, 2FA apps, your email inbox as master key.
  • Questions: Is there one email account that can reset almost everything?
  • Are your passwords truly unique or only small variations?

2.3 Human layer

  • You under stress, family, trusted people.
  • Questions: Does anyone know you even have a wallet?
  • Is there a clean way for someone to step in if you’re in hospital or unable to act?
Key phrase:
“Security is not only tech – it’s mainly clarity about who can do what in an emergency.”

Step 3

Seeds & wallets – secure, but not lost

3.1 Typical seed mistakes

Module 5 builds on Module 3 and goes deeper into the backup topic. Typical pitfalls:

  • Seed on a single paper sheet in a drawer with no protection.
  • Seed only in a password manager with no physical backup.
  • Seed photographed and stored in the cloud.
  • Seed hidden in such a smart way that even you might not find it later.

Reflection: “If you had to restore your seed in 2 years – with less memory than today – would you find it and understand it?”

3.2 Backup strategy: simple and robust

The course doesn’t push any brand, just principles:

  • At least one physical backup outside your main residence.
  • Clear format: readable, complete, not half-encrypted riddles.
  • Separation of seed and labels.

Typical building blocks: paper backup, metal backup, a combination with parts of the information at a second location.

Mini task: “Describe in one sentence how your main seed is stored today – and what would happen to it in a house fire.”

Step 4

Passwords, 2FA & email – your master key

4.1 Email as the real root access

Your primary email account is often the true root:

  • Password resets for many services.
  • 2FA backup codes and recovery links.
  • Communication hub for banks, exchanges and tools.

So you need a strong, unique password here, 2FA and securely stored backup codes.

4.2 Password strategy

Again, no product promotion – just logic you can stick to long term:

  • No reuse of the same password across crypto, email and banking.
  • Clear separation between everyday passwords and critical passwords.
  • A system you will still understand in five years.

Mini task: “In your private notes, list three accounts whose passwords you want to review or upgrade first.”

Step 5

Emergency access for trusted people

Module 5 also asks: “What happens to your funds if something happens to you?” This is not legal advice, but practical options.

  • You define one person or two who may know, in an emergency, that you hold crypto.
  • That person knows roughly where information can be found.
  • References can live in your important documents or in your will.

Mini task: “Answer for yourself: Who should know in an emergency that I hold crypto? Where could this person find a first hint?”

Step 6

Testing restore – without panic

Security without testing stays theory. In this step you plan a small restore test:

  • Create a small test wallet with a tiny amount.
  • Document the process: write down the seed → lose the device → restore the wallet on a second device.
  • Observe: How long does it take? Which information was missing? When did you feel nervous?

Note here: “My estimated time to restore a wallet from backup is: ____ minutes.”
