Crypto Glossary – Terms for conscious decisions

This glossary collects key terms from the crypto space with a focus on understanding, security and decision quality. Some entries overlap with classic trading; for deeper strategy aspects you can use the Trading Glossary.

Note: orientation tool only, not financial advice.

1 · Fundamentals & structure

Terms that help you read the map: why we have coins, tokens, Layer 1 and Layer 2 – and how networks connect.

Blockchain
Foundation

A blockchain is a distributed ledger: data is stored in blocks, linked with cryptography and validated by many nodes.

Academy view: the record book – but not every entry is automatically a good investment.
Coin
Asset type

A coin is the native asset of its own chain (BTC, ETH, …), used to pay fees and secure the network.

Academy view: coins are the “operating system money” of a network.
Token
Asset type

A token runs on top of an existing chain (for example ERC-20) and can represent access, voting power or rewards.

Academy view: a guest on someone else’s infrastructure – the logic behind it matters.
Layer 1 / Layer 2
Network layer

Layer 1 is the main chain; Layer 2 solutions sit on top and bundle or offload transactions to reduce cost and load.

Academy view: Layer 1 is the highway, Layer 2 the express lane above it.
Bridge
Infrastructure

A bridge connects networks so that value can move between chains.

Academy view: bridges are convenient – and among the most frequently attacked spots.

2 · Wallets, keys & security

Where sovereignty becomes real: if these terms are unclear, moving large amounts is not a good idea yet.

Wallet
Security

A wallet manages your private keys and signs transactions. Coins remain on the blockchain itself.

Academy view: your wallet is your keyring, not your vault.
Seed phrase
Core protection

The seed phrase (12–24 words) is the master key from which your private keys are derived.

Academy view: whoever has the seed can rebuild your wallet anywhere on earth.
Hot / cold wallet
Wallet type

Hot wallet: online, convenient, more attack surface. Cold wallet: offline, for long-term storage of larger amounts.

Academy view: hot = daily wallet, cold = vault – both are part of a setup.
Custodial / non-custodial
Control

Custodial: a service holds your keys. Non-custodial: you hold them yourself.

Academy view: “not your keys, not your coins” is a legal fact, not a slogan.

3 · DeFi, stablecoins & NFTs

Terms that explain why protocols talk about yields, pools and digital collectibles – and where the hidden risks sit.

Smart contract
DeFi

A smart contract is program code on-chain that executes rules automatically once conditions are met.

Academy view: a contract without human judgement – and without goodwill if something breaks.
Gas fees
Fees

Gas fees are the transaction costs of a network. They depend on traffic and complexity of your action.

Academy view: gas is the rent you pay for computation on the chain.
Liquidity pool
DeFi

A liquidity pool is a shared pot of tokens that enables decentralised swaps on a DEX.

Academy view: you change from trader to “liquidity provider” – with a different risk profile.
Stablecoin
Value buffer

A stablecoin aims to track a stable reference (often 1 USD) and is used as a parking spot between fiat and crypto.

Academy view: tool for liquidity and parking – not automatically a safe savings account.
NFT
Ownership & access

An NFT (non-fungible token) represents a unique item such as art, tickets or access rights.

Academy view: more about new ownership models than guaranteed profits.
Meme coin
Speculation

A meme coin is mostly driven by jokes and hype, often with little underlying business logic.

Academy view: if used at all, it belongs in your learning budget, not your safety zone.

4 · Overlap: crypto & trading

Classic trading terms that show up everywhere in crypto. For strategies and practical use, the Trading Glossary and Trading Awareness page go deeper.

Volatility
Market behaviour · trading

Volatility describes how strongly prices move. Crypto markets are usually highly volatile.

Academy view: something you plan for, not something that “just happens”.
Market / limit order
Order type

Market: execute now at the current price. Limit: execute only at your chosen price or better.

Academy view: market = speed, limit = control – both need written rules.
Stop-loss
Risk limit

A stop-loss sell order triggers when price falls below a level, aiming to cap losses.

Academy view: a tool against panic, not a magic shield – more in the Trading Glossary.
Leverage
Trading risk

Leverage means trading with borrowed funds, magnifying gains and losses.

Academy view: belongs to the advanced corner, not the beginner area.
Penny stocks & “cheap coins”
Speculation

Penny stocks are very cheap, often illiquid shares. In crypto, extremely cheap tokens behave similarly.

Academy view: “cheap” is not the same as “good value” – a strict risk budget is key.
