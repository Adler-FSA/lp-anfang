6 Crypto Setup – Module

Module 6 – Daily Routines & Warning Signals

Your setup should not only look good on day one, but stay stable in everyday life.

After this module you know which routines keep your setup healthy, which warning signals you should take seriously, and how to adjust your behavior before things become critical.

Step 1

Mini scenario: “The setup that slowly erodes”

Imagine you built a clean crypto setup — everything distributed, documented, seeds secured. Then everyday life happens: work, family, projects.

After 6–12 months you notice:

  • One password was quickly reused.
  • A new account was added, but never entered into your overview.
  • An important note exists only inside a phone chat history.

The setup is not suddenly broken — it slowly frays.

Question: “Do you notice a tendency to note things for later — and never cleanly add them back into your system?”

Step 2

Three routine layers: Overview – Security – Emotion

Module 6 builds on all previous modules, but focuses on consistency. We work with three layers:

  • Overview routine – do you still know what you own and where it sits?
  • Security routine – are your protection layers still up to date?
  • Emotional routine – is your behavior driven by your goals or by your feelings?

Which of these layers feels strongest today — and which needs the most attention?

Step 3

Overview routines: your finance check-in

3.1 Monthly mini review (10–20 minutes)

A simple routine suggestion, once a month:

  • Quickly open your overview from Modules 1–3.
  • Check:
    • Any new accounts, cards or wallets?
    • Did you connect any new services?
    • Has your overall picture shifted too strongly?

You answer: “Do I feel comfortable with my current distribution — or do I see a concentration I never actually wanted?”

3.2 Quarterly check: does your setup still match your goals?

Every 3 months:

  • Take a short look at your goals from Modules 2 & 3.
  • Questions:
    • Has your life situation changed?
    • Do reserves or diversification need to be adjusted?

Mini task: “Define a fixed slot that suits you.”

Step 4

Security routines: small checks instead of major panic

4.1 Yearly security reset

Topics:

  • Device check
  • Password check
  • Review 2FA settings

What do you want to specifically check or change during your next security reset?

4.2 Seed & backup status

Questions to ask yourself 1–2 times a year:

  • Do I know today where my most important seed is located?
  • Has my storage situation changed?
  • Do I need a second backup location?

Mini task: “Briefly note whether you are satisfied with your current seed backup — and one thing you want to improve within the next 30 days.”

Step 5

Emotional routines: your shield against impulsive action

Crypto and decentralization are not only technical topics — they trigger emotions: fear, greed, FOMO, uncertainty.

5.1 A stop sign for spontaneous actions

Module 6 introduces one principle:

“No structural intervention without the overnight rule.”
You do not spontaneously change your entire setup, your wallet structure or your reserve strategy just because of one message, one tweet or one video.

Instead, you write the urge down, sleep on it for at least one night and check the next day whether it still seems sensible.

What could your personal overnight rule look like in practice?

5.2 Knowing your personal triggers

Questions in the course:

  • What is most likely to push you into hectic actions?
  • What would be your countermeasure?

Mini task: “Write a personal sentence: ‘When I notice X, I do not make spontaneous changes — I first do Y.’”

Step 6

Warning signals — when you should take action

Module 6 names situations where you should actively respond:

  • You have accounts or devices that you barely use anymore, but which still control significant amounts of money.
  • There are new legal or tax rules in your country.
  • A platform starts attracting attention through outages or legal problems.
  • You notice disorder in your own notes and access structure.

Suggestion: “If at least two of these warning signals appear at the same time, take 60–90 minutes within the next 7 days to clean up your setup.”
