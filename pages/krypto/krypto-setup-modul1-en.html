1 Crypto Setup – Module

Inventory & Risk Profile

In this module you clean up your financial picture: what you own, where your accounts sit and how much protection you realistically need. You turn gut feeling into numbers – as the foundation for all further steps in your crypto setup.

Goal: You know where your money lives today, which risks you are carrying and how much volatility you can actually afford – without derailing your life.

This module is intentionally simple. You don’t need jargon – just honesty with yourself and a bit of time to write things down.
Note: This module is part of the protected crypto setup. If you haven’t entered the mentor password yet, it’s best to start via the setup overview.

Step 1

Inventory of your financial world

Before you think about wallets, DeFi or security, you need a clear picture: Where is what today? Without inventory, any setup only feels “secure”.

1.1 Collect accounts & positions

Write down all positions – rough amounts are enough:

  • Current and savings accounts (incl. overdrafts)
  • Savings plans, brokerage accounts, building-society contracts, insurance with balance
  • Cash, precious metals, crypto holdings (including on exchanges!)
  • Liabilities: loans, instalment plans, unpaid taxes, private loans

1.2 Who really controls what?

For each position, note: Who is in control? You, a bank, a broker, an employer? This is where the later separation between central and decentral setup begins.

  • “Account at Bank X” → bank can block, garnish, limit.
  • “Coins on Exchange Y” → platform risk, access risk, KYC.
  • “Wallet on my own device” → you are the key – and the risk.
Key thought: The more central something is, the easier third parties can reach it. The more decentralized something is, the more responsibility sits with you.

Short notes: what is “central” vs “decentral” in your world today?

Step 2

Your personal protection needs

Your risk profile has two sides: what you can emotionally tolerate and what you objectively must carry.

2.1 Three questions for your risk profile

  • How do you react if your net worth temporarily drops −30% in value?
  • How long could you bridge things with no income – only with reserves?
  • Which number would be an “existential shock” if it suddenly disappeared?

Mini task: “Risk scale” – pick a number from 1 (very cautious) to 10 (very risk-hungry) + your reason:

2.2 Protection against real threats

The most relevant risks for a crypto setup:

  • Bank account freezes or garnishments
  • Exchange insolvencies or hacks
  • Loss of access data, seed phrase or devices
  • Overreaching regulation or sudden bans

Which 1–2 risks feel most real to you right now?

2.3 Your “non-negotiable” area

Define an amount or share that is not for trading or speculation, but purely for security and basic needs.

  • e.g. 3–6 months of living expenses as a safety buffer
  • no short-term experiments with rent, insurance or tax reserves

Write your sentence, e.g.: “This X € / $ is only for real emergencies – not for trades, not for FOMO.”

Step 3

Typical pitfalls in the inventory phase

Many people don’t underestimate risk – they underestimate mess. Common mistakes:

  • Several accounts, but nobody knows what each is actually for.
  • Crypto accounts on 3–4 exchanges with small amounts – but no overview.
  • Passwords in your head, on sticky notes or in the browser instead of a clean system.
  • No clear separation between money for today, for later and for experiments.

Cleanup steps

  • Give every account a role: everyday, reserve, investing, experiment.
  • Decide which exchanges or accounts you could close or consolidate.
  • Start a password overview (e.g. password manager) – without raw details scattered everywhere.

Step 4

What you take away from Module 1

By the end of this module, you should:

have a list of your key accounts, portfolios and crypto holdings.
know which positions are centrally controlled and which are under your own keys.
be able to roughly describe your personal risk profile.
know an amount you don’t want to touch.
