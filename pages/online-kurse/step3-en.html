Step 3 – Security & Self-Custody

Now it’s about control – not out of fear, but out of responsibility. The one who holds the keys owns the money.

Mentor note: “Convenience is the enemy of sovereignty.”

Why this step matters

You are leaving the comfort zone of purely centralised services. Your goal is to take back control – without panic, but with a system. This step is about seeds, passphrases and hardware wallets.

Seeds – real ownership

Your seed (recovery phrase) is your access to everything. It consists of 12 or 24 words that generate your private keys. Anyone who knows these words controls your money – on any device.

Two concepts:

  • Seed phrase (12 / 24 words): generated during wallet setup – usually BIP-39 standard.
  • Passphrase (“25th word”): free extra phrase that protects you – anyone with seed only will see an empty wallet.

Mentor note: “The seed is the key – the passphrase is the lock in front of it.”

More about the passphrase (“25th word”)

The passphrase is not an official BIP-39 25th word, but an additional input. Your 12/24 words and the passphrase together form a new master seed – generating different wallet addresses.

  • Same seed + different passphrase = new wallet.
  • If you forget the passphrase, there is no recovery.
  • Different wallets handle special characters slightly differently – testing is essential.
  • Hardware wallets like Ledger, Trezor, Coldcard and BitBox02 support this officially.
  • Always test on a second device before moving larger amounts.

Wallets with 24 words + passphrase option

Some wallets support 24-word seeds and an additional passphrase. This is ideal if you plan to keep larger amounts safely over the long term.

Wallet Seed length Special feature Type Extra protection
Ledger Nano X 24 words BIP-39 standard Hardware optional 25th word

Coldcard Mk4 24 fully offline, “duress” mode Hardware 25th word / decoy wallet

MEW (MyEtherWallet) 12 / 24 non-custodial, can sign via hardware Software no own passphrase feature, but works with hardware wallets
Sparrow / Electrum / BlueWallet 12 / 24 BIP-39 compatible Desktop / mobile passphrase-ready (depending on wallet)

Tip: Check if your wallet uses BIP-39 – only then can seeds be reused across devices.

Safe storage & contingency plan

  • Write down 24 words by hand, no screenshots.
  • Store seed and device in different locations.
  • Use fire-/water-resistant seed plates.
  • Use a second “practice wallet” with small funds.
  • Perform a recovery test on a spare device.

Mentor quote: “You can replace a wallet – you can’t replace a seed.”
